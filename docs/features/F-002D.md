# F-002D: Combat Predicate Characterization

## Objective

Characterize low-coupling combat movement predicates before broader combat or autocombat work.

## User Value

Combat movement and collision behavior must survive the port. These predicates provide a bounded entry point into combat rules.

## Non-Goals

- Do not characterize the full combat loop.
- Do not port rendering, sound, projectiles, victory, or monster AI.
- Do not include `ValidMove` until sound effects are stubbed or isolated.

## Acceptance Notes

- Characterization covers `CombatValidMove`, `CombatMonsterHere`, and `CombatCharacterHere`, or documents deferrals.
- Tests populate synthetic combat globals and assert exact return codes and lookup priority.
- Monster type/passability cases and reverse-iteration collision behavior are covered.

## Result

Status: complete as of 2026-05-30.

The harness lives in `harness/combat_predicates_tests.c` and covers:

- `CombatValidMove`
- `CombatMonsterHere`
- `CombatCharacterHere`

It uses synthetic `gMonType`, `MonsterX`, `MonsterY`, `MonsterHP`, `CharX`, and `CharY` state. It covers:

- land-style monster passability for tiles `2`, `4`, `6`, and `0x10`
- the `0x16` through `0x1F` monster type range that only accepts tile `0`
- `0x20` and higher monster types returning to land-style passability
- ignored dead monster slots
- duplicate monster coordinates returning the lowest matching live slot
- duplicate character coordinates returning the highest matching slot
- character lookup not checking health or active status

`ValidMove` remains out of scope because it plays audio; F-002D did not require it.
